<p><p>The biosurfactants market is segmented into Rhamnolipids, Sophorolipids, and other types. Rhamnolipids, produced mainly by Pseudomonas aeruginosa, are known for their strong emulsifying and antimicrobial properties, making them ideal for industrial applications. Sophorolipids, derived from yeasts like Candida bombicola, exhibit excellent surface activity and biodegradability, often utilized in personal care and cleaning products. Other biosurfactants include mannosylerythritol lipids and lipopeptides, serving diverse sectors such as agriculture, food, and pharmaceuticals due to their eco-friendly characteristics.</p></p>
